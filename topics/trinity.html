How can God be 3 and 1 at the same time?

The #1 Muslim (and atheist) objection to the Trinity — answered so your kid can explain it in the group chat.

The objection you’ll hear on TikTok and from Muslim friends:
“You Christians say Father + Son + Holy Spirit = one God? 1 + 1 + 1 = 3 gods! That’s math, not monotheism. You’re polytheists.”

1. It’s one “WHAT” and three “WHOs” (the apple illustration everyone gets)

An apple has three parts that are all 100% apple, yet they’re distinct:

  • Skin – fully apple
  • Flesh – fully apple
  • Core – fully apple

They’re not ⅓ apple each. Each part is completely apple, yet there aren’t three apples — just one apple.

God is one Being (one “WHAT” = divine nature) in three Persons (three “WHOs” = Father, Son, Holy Spirit). All three are fully God, yet there is only one God.

2. Three drops of water become ONE drop (same essence, different persons)

Take three separate drops of water and touch them together — they instantly become one single drop. Same substance (H₂O), yet moments before they were three distinct drops.

Father, Son, and Spirit are three distinct Persons who share the exact same divine essence. They never “merge” into one Person, but they are never separate Gods either — perfectly one in nature.

3. The knockout: The Essence–Energy Distinction (Eastern Orthodox clarity)

God’s ESSENCE (what He is) is utterly one, simple, unknowable.
God’s ENERGIES (how He acts and relates) are uncreated and can be three Persons without dividing the essence.

St. Gregory Palamas (14th century) showed this isn’t some new idea — it’s how the early church always understood it. Islam and Western logic often collapse “essence” and “energy” together, but Scripture and the fathers don’t. We worship one God in three Persons, not three gods or one Person wearing masks.

Key verses to memorize & fire back

  • Matthew 28:19 – “baptize them in the name [singular] of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit”
  • John 10:30 – Jesus: “I and the Father are one.”
  • John 15:26 – The Spirit “proceeds from the Father” (distinct Person)
  • 2 Corinthians 13:14 – All three Persons in one blessing
Bottom line your kid can text back:
“It’s not 1+1+1=3. It’s 1×1×1=1. One divine nature, three eternal Persons. Just like one apple has skin, flesh, and core — all 100% apple, yet still one apple.”
